As you may know, I am professionaly seen as a .NET developer which means I'm also included in the Microsoft's stack of people working with their technologies.
While I am not only using Microsoft's technologies, I am more experienced with it thus this post will give examples in C# but nothing prevents you from applying the same structure in your JavaScript, Python or Java projects!

Not long ago, I have been asked by fellow developers how I structure my projects because when they evolve, the basic N-tier architecture becomes more complex and they tend to create messy solutions.
I don't have all the answers but when it comes to my own projects, I use what Robert C. Martin called [the Clean Architecture](https://blog.cleancoder.com/uncle-bob/2012/08/13/the-clean-architecture.html) in 2012.
Of course, my version is not strictly following what Uncle Bob describes in his blog as I have tailored it to my needs (actually, people did it for me) but let's talk about it.

**What does the Clean Architecture bring me that I love and that N-tier does not give me?**
